This report presents a comprehensive study on biomedical implant materials and 
their properties, with a focus on the TiNi shape memory alloy (SMA). TiNi SMA is 
chosen for investigation due to its exceptional attributes including high corrosion 
resistance, strength, low modulus of elasticity, and superior shape recovery capabilities. 
However, conventional machining methods struggle with TiNi SMA due to its unique 
characteristics, necessitating the exploration of various non-conventional machining 
processes. This research centres on micro wire electric discharge machining (microWEDM).
The optimization of process parameters for micro-WEDM of TiNi smart alloy is 
a key aspect of this study. Responses to different process parameters such as voltage, 
capacitance, and wire feed rate are measured against output parameters like surface 
roughness, material removal rate, kerf width, and dimensional deviation. The 
optimization process employs a combined approach of Taguchi L27 orthogonal array 
(OA) and Grey Relational Analysis (GRA). Taguchi methodology is utilized to design an 
OA, followed by experimental trials on micro-WEDM. Analysis of variance (ANOVA) 
is then conducted to assess the impact of each process parameter on the output parameters.
Furthermore, this report includes ANSYS simulations of a 3.5 recon plate. The 
recon plate and human bone are modelled through reverse engineering using 3D scanning 
techniques. Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) is utilized to examine both existing 
and newly developed models. A comparative analysis between the newly developed 
model using optimized parameters and the existing model is discussed.
Overall, this report offers insights into the machining of TiNi SMA using microWEDM, as well as the simulation and analysis of biomedical implants, contributing to 
the advancement of implant materials and manufacturing techniques.
